Meet

Dr. Magdelene Amoateng (MD)

Magdelene Amoateng (MD) is a dedicated primary care physician with extensive experience in patient-centered care, specializing in palliative care and public health. Completing a residency where she served as Chief Privacy Officer, Magdelene Amoateng (MD) advocated for resident wellness during the COVID-19 pandemic, ensuring that concerns were heard and addressed.

While pursuing a Master of Public Health (MPH), Magdelene Amoateng (MD) developed a strong understanding of health promotion and disease prevention, which has effectively informed her medical practice. Following residency, she further honed her skills through a palliative care fellowship at MD Anderson Cancer Center, cultivating expertise in end-of-life care, symptom management, and effective communication with patients and their caregivers.

Magdelene Amoateng (MD) worked as a primary care physician at Sanitas Medical Center in Katy, Texas, where she delivered exceptional care to patients with acute and chronic conditions, emphasizing preventive medicine and age-appropriate screenings. Her collaborative approach included working closely with other health providers and specialists to create integrated care plans that prioritizes patient well-being.

She also worked as a Clinical Specialist then moved on to being a Hospitalist at M.D. Anderson Cancer Center, focused on ensuring that cancer patients received thorough and compassionate care. She actively engaged in interdisciplinary collaboration and education for medical students, fellows, and clinical providers, fostering an environment of continuous learning and knowledge advancement in healthcare.

In addition to her professional commitments, Magdelene Amoateng (MD) enjoys reading, singing, cooking, and spending quality time with family. These hobbies provide a balance to her busy career and inspire her dedication to improving community health.

With a commitment to enhancing patient outcomes and overall wellness, Magdelene Amoateng (MD) aspires to implement her expertise in palliative care and public health to serve the populations she works with effectively.
